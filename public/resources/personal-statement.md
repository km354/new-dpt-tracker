# Personal Statement Guide

## Overview

Your personal statement is a critical component of your DPT program application. It's your opportunity to tell your story and demonstrate your passion for physical therapy.

## Key Components

### 1. Introduction
- Start with a compelling hook that captures the reader's attention
- Clearly state your interest in physical therapy
- Mention what drew you to this field

### 2. Body Paragraphs
- Share specific experiences that shaped your interest
- Discuss relevant volunteer work or observation hours
- Highlight your understanding of the PT profession
- Connect your experiences to your future goals

### 3. Conclusion
- Summarize your key points
- Reiterate your commitment to the profession
- Express enthusiasm for the program

## Tips for Success

- **Be specific**: Use concrete examples rather than generic statements
- **Show, don't tell**: Demonstrate your qualities through stories
- **Stay within word limits**: Most programs have specific requirements
- **Proofread carefully**: Errors can undermine your message
- **Be authentic**: Let your genuine passion shine through

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Writing a generic statement that could apply to any program
- Focusing too much on your past instead of your future goals

- Exceeding word or character limits
- Submitting without thorough editing
